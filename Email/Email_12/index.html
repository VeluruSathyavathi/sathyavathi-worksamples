New Data Leaks Call Twitter and ChatGPT’s Safety into Question
 
Earlier this week, it was announced that parts of Twitter’s source code had been leaked online, making the platform more vulnerable to competitors and hackers who could use the information for future attacks. Add on a bug that leaked email addresses and other information of ChatGPT users, and we’ve got another week of 🚩🚩🚩 surrounding data collection and privacy.
 
The Bottom Line.
 
Data leaks are not uncommon, but it is important to monitor how transparent platforms are when it comes to the steps these companies are taking to protect their users and mitigate future risks, especially when it comes to personal data. Cue the “consumer trust” brand apology tour.
 
More Than Meets The Eye: Understanding Influencers on a Deeper Level
 
We tend to categorize creators, celebs, and influencers into niches, but true authenticity can come from partnerships that tap into after-hours passions and personas. For instance, would you have expected beauty queen Sydney Sweeney to be a lifelong car mechanic? Ford did.
 
The Bottom Line.
 
Any beauty brand can put Sydney Sweeney in makeup. But only Ford can tap into her dream of restoring and driving a vintage Bronco. Finding a deeper insight to guide who you choose to represent your brand works to build real relationships and content that resonates.
